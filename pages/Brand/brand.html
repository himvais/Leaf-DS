Values

Professional

ERM business tools play an important decision in a lot of buisness decision making. In order to create tools on which experts can rely on - we need to show examplry professionalism.

Consistent

Many of our clients use multiple ERM tools at the same time. Providing a consistent experience across multiple products and over all channels (Product UX, Email Communications & documents) creates an unparalleled brand impact.

User Centric

All of our product design deliverables starts with keeping our end-users at the center. This applies to our customer facing products as well as the ERM internal tools we create!

Practical

We only get a small window to deliver ideas as usable products, this means all the design decisions we make should be precise, implementable and easily understood by development team.

Voice & Tone

Voice & Tone principles are our way to convey our personality and mood and embed it into all product copy. All product copy, micro-copy and prompts should align to these principles.

Informing

We should use clear and precise messaging using simple words. We should convey every piece of imformation with confidence with providing further actions.

When to use: When users are feeling confused, annoyed or cornered for example: New users, Error Messenging etc.

Inspiring

We should use a supportive and educating tone while gently guiding users to complete certain actions.

When to use: When users are feeling confident and anticipating next steps for example, Long workflows and forms.

Encourging

We should use a very human tone while still maintaining a dependable and confident approach for this type of messaging.

When to use: When users are feeling exhausted, tiered or lost for example, onboarding, error-messaging, long-form actions.

Delight

We should use a light, encouraging and affirmative language for all the positive use cases to create delight.

When to use: When users are feeling accomplished, confident and joy for example, success messages, completions messages and positive outcomes.
